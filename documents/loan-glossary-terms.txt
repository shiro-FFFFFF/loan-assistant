# Loan Glossary and Terms

## A-D

**Amortization**: The process of paying off a loan through regular payments over time, with each payment covering both principal and interest.

**Annual Percentage Rate (APR)**: The total yearly cost of a loan including interest rate plus fees, expressed as a percentage.

**Asset**: Property or belongings that have value and can be used as collateral for loans.

**Balloon Payment**: A large payment due at the end of a loan term.

**Borrowed Funds**: Money received from a lender that must be repaid with interest.

**Capitalization**: Adding unpaid interest to the principal balance of a loan.

**Collateral**: Property or assets pledged as security for a loan.

**Co-signer**: Person who signs a loan agreement and becomes responsible for repayment if the primary borrower defaults.

**Credit Check**: Investigation of a borrower's credit history and credit score.

**Credit Score**: A numerical rating (300-850) that represents a person's creditworthiness.

**Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)**: The percentage of monthly income that goes toward debt payments.

**Default**: Failure to make loan payments according to the terms of the loan agreement.

## E-M

**Equity**: The difference between what a property is worth and what is owed on it.

**Escrow**: Money held by a third party to pay taxes and insurance on behalf of a borrower.

**Fixed-Rate Loan**: A loan with an interest rate that remains constant throughout the loan term.

**Foreclosure**: Legal process where a lender takes possession of property when borrower fails to make payments.

**Grace Period**: Time allowed after a payment due date before late fees are charged.

**Guarantee**: Promise to repay a loan if the primary borrower defaults.

**Hard Inquiry**: Credit check that affects credit score and appears on credit report.

**Interest**: The cost of borrowing money, expressed as a percentage of the loan amount.

**Interest Rate**: The percentage charged on outstanding loan balance.

** Lien**: Legal claim against property to secure payment of a debt.

**Loan-to-Value Ratio (LTV)**: The ratio of the loan amount to the value of the property being purchased.

**Maturity Date**: The date when a loan must be fully repaid.

**Mortgagor**: The borrower in a mortgage agreement.

**Mortgagee**: The lender in a mortgage agreement.

## N-Z

**Negative Amortization**: When monthly payments aren't enough to cover interest, causing the loan balance to increase.

**Origination Fee**: Fee charged by lender for processing a new loan application.

**Prepayment Penalty**: Fee charged for paying off a loan before the scheduled maturity date.

**Principal**: The original amount of money borrowed, excluding interest.

**Refinancing**: Replacing an existing loan with a new loan, often with better terms.

**Secured Loan**: A loan backed by collateral that can be seized if borrower defaults.

**Soft Inquiry**: Credit check that doesn't affect credit score and doesn't appear on credit report.

**Term**: The length of time over which a loan must be repaid.

**Unsecured Loan**: A loan not backed by collateral, based only on borrower's creditworthiness.

**Variable-Rate Loan**: A loan with an interest rate that can change over time.

**Write-off**: When a lender removes a bad debt from their books, often after collection attempts fail.

**Yield**: The return on investment for a lender, equivalent to the interest rate received.
